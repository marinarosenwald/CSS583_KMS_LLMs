In this chapter, the cycle of information retrieval and recycling is introduced. It is based on the principle that knowledge that moves forward in time is worth more than knowledge that sits idle. Knowledge acquisition and recycling takes place through a number of steps called the "KMII" in the information-retrieval cycle. The stages of the KMII are defined in much detail by Meyer and Zuck in 1996. Here, they describe how information is acquired, developed, stored, used, and sold to customers in a variety of industries.
